Question 11

As the upper extremity swings forward, the hand (H) rotates about the shoulder (S). In the situation shown below the length of the limb is 0.6 m and the angular velocity of the limb (ω) is 18 rad/s. As the hand rotates about the shoulder, the shoulder has a linear velocity of 2 m/s in the horizontal direction. What is the magnitude of the velocity of the hand with respect to the the ground (VH/G) if θ = 65 degrees?

Solution Section:

Since the hand is rotating about the shoulder while the shoulder is translating relative to the ground, the velocity of the hand relative to the ground is the vector sum of the hand velocity relative to the shoulder plus the shoulder velocity relative to the ground.
