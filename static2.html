Static Equilibrium of Rigid Bodies (2-D)

The motion (or lack of motion) of any body is caused by a system of forces.

A rigid body is in static equilibrium
when the sum of all the forces and
moments acting on it are equivalent
to zero. Which means that:

ΣFx = 0
ΣFy = 0
ΣM = 0

In order to make the calculations in the above three equations, we must first resolve each of the forces into X and Y components and calculate the moment of each force about the axis.
