Anthropometry

In order to analyze the mechanics of anything, we must know the dimensions of its components. This is more easily done if you are analyzing man made objects but it is considerably more difficult when it comes to analyzing the human body.

Anthropometry is the science that deals with the measurement of size, mass, shape and inertial properties of the human body.

In this part of the course we will look at some very basic dimensions of the human body. These dimensions are often called body segment parameters (BSPs) and the list includes:

- total body height
- total body mass
- body segment lengths
- body segment masses
- body segment centers of gravity
- total body center of gravity
- body segment moments of inertia
- body segment radii of gyration

Body segment lengths can be measured fairly easily and accurately on living human subjects by using a cloth tape measure. If we make the simplifying assumption that all humans have similar segment length ratios of their whole body height, we can use the figure below.

The total body height is represented by "H" and the dimension of length of important segments is given by the portion of H.

Example: Find the forearm length of a person with a total body height of 1.8 m.

Solution: Length = 0.146H = 0.146(1.8)= 0.263 m

Example: Find the thigh length of a person who has a height of 1.58 m.

Solution: Length = 0.53H - 0.285H = 0.245(1.58)= 0.387 m
