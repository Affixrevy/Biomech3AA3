Question 10

A hot air balloon has a volume of 2800 m3 when fully inflated. The mass of the balloon, basket, burner and fuel tanks is 280 kg. If the density of the hot air is 0.95 kg/m3 and the density of the outside air is 1.2 kg/m3, how much extra mass can be carried in the basket?

Correct. Try Leading Question 3.

Solution Section:

We use the density of the hot air to find it's mass (0.95 times 2800) and add the mass of the rest of the balloon to get the total mass (2940 kg). We then multiply the mass by the gravitational constant (9.81 m/s2) to get the weight of the balloon inflated with hot air.
