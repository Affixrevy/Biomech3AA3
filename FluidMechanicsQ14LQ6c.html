Question 14

Consider a box with the dimensions of 1.0 m x 0.3 m x 0.3 m and the box has a density of 160 kg/m3. An iron weight with the dimensions of 0.1 x 0.1 m x 0.1 m is placed in the middle at the bottom of the box. If the density of the iron weight is 7800 kg/m3, how far does the box sink into the fresh water (h)?

Solution Section:

We previously determined the density of the box with the weight inside (247 kg/m3). We now use the density of water to find the percentage of the volume of the box that must be submerged in order for it to displace enough water to create a buoyant force that is equal to its weight. In this case, the height of the box is 0.3 m and it will sink down to the same ratio of its density to the water's density.
