Strain Energy

It is often easier to consider objects to be rigid. We often consider the human body to be a system of rigid segments because of the bones within them. However, some objects are quite deformable and their shapes change dramatically under the influence of force. If we examine a spring or rubber band, we find that the more force we apply, the greater its deformation. For most materials, the deformation is directly proportional to the force causing the deformation. That is, if the force doubles, so too does the deformation.
