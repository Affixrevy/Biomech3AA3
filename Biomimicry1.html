Modeling, Understanding and Biomimicry

I have long held the belief that our understanding of nature is reflected by our ability to model it. If we can construct a model (either physical or mathematical) that can behave exactly the way nature behaves under all possible conditions, then we fully understand the phenomenon.

Black Box Models vs Conceptual Models:

A black box model is one in which we would like to predict an outcome but the causes and the process is unimportant. A neural network is an example as are statistical models.

Future Direction:

Robert Full stated that biomimicry is not what we should be attempting because copying without knowing the reason behind the phenomenon is not understanding. However, conceptual models that lead to biomimicry that behaves exactly the way nature behaves is our highest level of understanding.
