Lab Summary

In order to perform a biomechanical analysis, we must collect and process data.  The prior labs have taught the scientific method that guides the design of any experiment, the measurement chain that allows biomechanists to select the equipment, processing and analysis.  This lab puts it all together with a list of considerations and some examples to practice the planning.

Considerations

- Can I measure the phenomenon?

- Lab or Field setting?

- Human or animal subjects

- Kinematic Data Collection

    -2D or 3D

    - How much digitizing?

    - Subject encumberment

- Kinetic Data Collection

    - How many force platforms?

    - Strain Gauge instrumentation?

- EMG

    - type of electrodes

    - number of muscles measured

-Transducer Considerations

    - equipment specifications (sensitivity, linearity, hysteresis, precision, frequency response)

- Processing

    - computer sampling rate (camera filmimg rate)

    - filtering, differentiation, integration

- Subject Selection

- Statistical generalization. 

Example 1: Weight lifting belts prevent low back injury during occupational lifting tasks.

Example 2: What is the role of two joint muscles in the maximum vertical jump?.

Example 3: Motor vehicle occupant safety "What is the effect of air bags on reduced occupant impact"?
