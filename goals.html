Goals and Objectives of Biomechanists

This page is intended to give a more complete picture of the field of biomechanics than that provided by the definitions on the main page. This is not intended to be an exhaustive list of the entire field but it briefly describes the many ways in which biomechanical knowledge is used by those who have developed an expertise in this field.

1) Diagnosis of Movement Pathology: Clinical gait analysis, stroke

2) Facilitate Performance: biofeedback, athletic screening, neural vs peripheral causes

3) Reduce Risk of Injury: measurement of tissue tolerance, pre-employment screening, physical demands assessment

4) Assessment of Remedial Programs: rehab, surgery, athletic

5) Design and Assessment of Devices: prosthetics, orthotics, safety equipment, exercise and athletic equipment

6) Forensic Evidence and Expert Testimony: crime/accident scene discovery, witness account plausibility, insurance fraud
