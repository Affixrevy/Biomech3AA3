Question 19

A lifeguard needs to get to a potential drowning victim as fast as possible. The victim is 80 m directly offshore and 30 meters along a rocky shoreline. The shortest distance to the victim would be to swim directly toward the victim (X = 30). However, the lifeguard can only swim at a velocity of 0.5 m/s and he can run along the shore at 1.6 m/s. Another strategy would be to run the 30 m along the shore and then swim 80 m (X = 0). A third strategy would be to run a partial distance (30 - X) and swim the rest. Which value of X allows the lifeguard to reach the victim in the least possible time?
