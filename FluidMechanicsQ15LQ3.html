Question 15

Consider a box with the dimensions of 1.0 m x 0.3 m x 0.3 m. An iron weight with the dimensions of 0.1 x 0.1 m x 0.1 m is placed at the bottom of the box. If the iron weight was placed in the middle of the box, the box would sink 0.1 m into the fresh water. However, the weight was shifted 0.2 m to the left causing the center of gravity (G) to shift 0.07 m to the left. This causes the box to tilt because the center of buoyancy (B) must act in the same vertical plane as G for static equilibrium. By definition, the center of buoyancy must have the same area on the left as on the right which means the box must tilt adding some buoyancy (area) to the left side and subtracting some buoyancy (area) from the right side. How much does the box tilt (θ)?
