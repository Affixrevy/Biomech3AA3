Question 16

The javelin thrower shown below is rotating his forearm about his elbow while his elbow is rotating about his shoulder and his shoulder is translating forward at 8.5 m/s. Use the given information to determine the direction of the velocity of his hand with respect to the the ground (VH/G).

Solution Section:

Since the elbow is rotating about the shoulder, the velocity of the elbow relative to the shoulder is a tangential velocity. Tangential velocity is directed at 90 degrees to the segment. In this case, the segment is 110 degrees from the positive X axis. Therefore, the arm is 20 degrees from vertical and the tangential velocity is 20 degrees above the positive X axis.
