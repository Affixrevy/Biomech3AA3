Question 14

A hockey player passes the puck to a fast skating teammate. The skater has a constant speed of 8 m/s and he must receive the puck before he reaches the offside line at 10 m. How fast must the puck be passed in order to complete the pass onside if the skater is 7 m ahead?

Solution Section:

We use the two displacement equations because both the puck and the skater need to have the same displacement (10 m) at the same time (t). Using the first equation, we find the time to be 0.375 seconds for the puck to reach the skater before the skater crosses the offside line. We then use this value for time to solve for the necessary puck velocity.
