Media Biography

James J. Dowling, Ph.D.

Dr. Dowling was born in Toronto, Canada. He attended Leaside High School and lettered in football, hockey and track and field. He was on the Dean's Honours List at the University of Windsor where he received his Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Human Kinetics and where he played varsity hockey and track and field. He received his Ph.D. specializing in the biomechanics of human motion from the University of Waterloo and he lectured at the University of Guelph and at Concordia University in Montreal before joining McMaster University in 1989.

He teaches undergraduate students and has supervised numerous Master's and Ph.D. students. He has published over 30 articles in international journals such as Journal of Biomechanics, Sports Medicine, Medicine and Science in Sports and Exercise and the European Journal of Applied Physiology and the Journal of Biomechanical Engineering. He presented regularly at national and international conferences and has been a keynote speaker for the International Society for the Biomechanics of Sport.

Dr. Dowling's research has been in the areas of muscle mechanics, human muscle power and modeling of the neuro-musculoskeletal system. Funding for this research has been provided by agencies such as the Natural Science and Engineering Council of Canada, Sick Children's Hospital Foundation and the Ontario Neurotrauma Foundation. The types of research have ranged from basic research to clinical applications on stroke victims, children with cerebral palsy, amputees and elite performances by athletes.

Dr. Dowling has been consulted by fitness companies as well as entrepreneurs and inventors concerning innovative products for exercise. He has also served as an expert witness in litigation concerning personal injury, insurance fraud as well as giving forensic biomechanics testimony in murder trials.
