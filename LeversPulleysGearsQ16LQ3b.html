Question 16

Consider the three gears on the left. Gear A has 60 teeth and gear C has 30 teeth. Gear B is a compound gear with 10 teeth attached in the middle of a larger gear with 42 teeth. If the gears are arranged in the manner on the right and gear A rotates at 100 rad/s, how fast will gear C rotate?

Solution Section:

We previously calculated the speed advantage of A vs B to be 60/10 and the speed advantage of B vs C to be 42/30. Therefore, we multiply these two ratios to get the speed advantage of A vs C. In this case, gear C will rotate 8.4 times for every rotation of gear A To calculate the speed of gear C, we multiply the speed of A (100 rad/s) by this factor.
