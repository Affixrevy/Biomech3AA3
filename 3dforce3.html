3-D Forces and Kinematics (continued)

Example of Rectangular components of a muscle force and direction cosines:

Consider a 500 N muscle force that inserts at (0.08, 0.0, -0.02) m and pulls toward its origin at (0.0, 0.20, 0.0) m as shown in the diagram to the right. Find the rectangular components of force and the direction cosines.

Solution:

The magnitude of each of the rectangular components of force is proportional to the length of the muscle in each of the three directions. Therefore, the first step requires us to find the muscle length and partition the force into each of the three rectangular components. Pythagoras is used to find the length and then each rectangular component is determined by dividing the muscle force (500 N) by the whole muscle length (L) and multiplying by the rectangular components of length. We must be careful of the sign of each force component. In this case, the force is acting at the insertion and is pulling toward the origin so the components of length are found by subtracting the origin coordinates from the insertion coordinates.
