Question 10

Consider the tightrope walker attempting to walk along a rope with his arms at his sides. If we neglect the arms, the rest of his body has a mass of 72 kg, a CofG located 1.04m directly above the rope and a moment of inertia about its CofG of 14 kg.m2. Each arm has a mass of 4 kg, a CofG location 1.1 m above the rope and 0.15m laterally from the rope. The moment of inertia of each arm about its CofG is 0.323 kg.m2. Find the total moment of inertia of the tightrope walker about the rope axis.
