Question 19

A metal seat with a mass of m2 = 32 kg hangs from one end of a rope that passes over a pulley with a coefficient of rolling friction of μ = 0.7. A spring scale hangs from the other end of a rope. A boy with mass m1 = 64 kg sits on the hung seat and holds himself in static equilibrium by pulling on the rope. What is the force reading of the scale that must be exceeded in order for the boy to raise himself? Ignore the mass of the scale, the rope and the pulley. The pulley has a radius of 0.20 m and an axle with a radius 0f 0.01 m.

Solution Section:

The lever method uses the distance "d" to shift the fulcrum to account for the rolling friction. We previously calculated d = 0.007 m. The boy exerts a force of S on the scale and the other end of the lever has the tension due to gravity acting on him and the seat (the radius of the pulley = 0.2 m). In order for him to raise himself, he must overcome gravity and rolling friction. Since friction opposes motion, the fulcrum is shifted toward "S" and away from "T" such that "S will have the smaller moment arm and require more force to overcome "T" and rolling friction.
