Question 18

A man pulls a heavy bus in a demonstration of strength. He knows that the friction between his shoes and the pavement is not high enough to keep his feet from slipping so he uses a rope and his arm strength to help pull the bus. The bus has a mass of 4400 kg and the rolling friction of the wheels has a "d" value of 0.035 m. The radius of the bus wheel is 0.44 m. The man exerts a force of C on the bus by attaching a cable at an angle of 20 degrees below horizontal and he exerts a horizontal force on the rope of R. His mass is 130 kg and the coefficient of friction between his shoes and the pavement is 0.61, find the force required by his arms (R).
