Question 10

A biomechanist wishes to know the amount of muscle force in the achilles tendon of a living animal without cutting the tendon. He uses a buckle force transducer which has three prongs that can be threaded around the tendon without removing the tendon from the bone. If the transducer measures a force of 253 N and the biomechanist knows the dimensions of the prongs, what is the muscle force (T1) if we consider the coefficient of friction to be 0.1 and the angle formed by the wrap around the first prong to be 20 degrees (β1 = 20o) and the wrap around the middle prong to be 40 degrees (β2 = 40o)?

The vertical components of T2 and T3 add up to be the 253 N measured force. The vertical components are the opposite sides of the 26.6o angle we calculated previously and we also found that T2 = 1.072T3 due to belt friction. Therefore the sum of the vertical components is T2 + 1/1.072 T2 = 1.933 T2 times sin26.6o.
