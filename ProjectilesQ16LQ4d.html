Question 16

A person throws a ball with an initial velocity of 20 m/s from a point located 2.0 m above the floor. Knowing that the ceiling of the gymnasium is 7.0 m high, determine the highest point at which the ball can strike the wall 20 m away.

Solution Section:

We know that vertical velocity is zero when the ball is at it's maximum height and that in order to strike the wall as high as possible, that maximum height must be 7 m. Therefore, we use the vertical velocity [2] and vertical displacement [3] equations to solve for the time and angle (θ) that allows the ball to reach maximum height when it is 7 m above the floor.. We used equation [2] to find an expression for t and then substituted this expression for t into equation [3] to find θ.
