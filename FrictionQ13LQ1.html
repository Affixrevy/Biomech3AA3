Question 13

A minor was trapped at the bottom of a mine shaft. He planned an escape by cutting two wood planks exactly 2.05 m in length. He only had four small nails to hammer into the sides of the wooden mine shaft. He estimated that two nails could only support 440 N so he planned to use friction to help hold the planks in place. His plan was to wedge one board against the walls, use two nails to help support the lower end and sit on it while he placed the next board above it. He was then going to climb to the top board and pull up the lower board, remove the two nails and continue the process until he reached the top. He knew that if he moved too close to the lower end of the board, it would fall so he needed to calculate how far (L) he could safely work without dislodging the board. If the coefficient of friction between the wood plank and the wood elevator shaft was 0.75, the weight of the board was 80 N, and his weight was 900 N, what is L?
