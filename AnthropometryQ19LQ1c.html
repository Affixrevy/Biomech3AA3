He used these girth measurements to yield the semi-major axes (b) and semi-minor axes (a) of a series of ellipses that would yield the volume of body segments, Once he had the volume of each slice (ellipse) he could estimate the mass of a segment and how the mass was distributed. The image below shows a subject with data for the axes of the ellipses for 12 slices that make up the head and neck segment. What is the volume of the 4th slice assuming that the volume of an ellipse is (a)(b)(π)(h) and h = 0.016 (all dimension are in meters)?
