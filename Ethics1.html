Ethics in Biomechanics Research

Much of what we know about biomechanics has been discovered or confirmed using human and animal research.  Every biomechanists either conducts research or uses the findings from research to practice biomechanics.  As such, it is a worthwhile exercise to develop a personal philosophy on the ethics of biomechanics research.  The purpose of this lab is not to dictate a particular philosophy.  The purpose is to inform students of the elements of informed consent used in human research and contrast animal with human research.

Informed Consent for Human Research:

An essential component of any research involving human subjects is the provision of informed consent.  Researchers must present a document to all and any subject that includes the major points below and obtain a signature proving the subject's agreement to participate in the experiment.  The major aspects include...

1) a description of all the procedures with a minimum of deception;

2) a clear presentation of risks, pain, discomfort. etc.;

3) a statement of the benefits the research will provide to others;

4) a reassuring statement of the steps taken to ensure the subject's privacy and confidentiality;

5) the option of an immediate withdrawal from the study at any point without repercussions and destruction of any data to that point.

Group Discussion

The class will divide into three groups.  Each group must do their best to formulate an argument in support of statement that they have been given.  You may not agree with the position but it is important to look at different points of view.  After each group has presented points in favor of each position, discussion can look at the merits and weight behind each. Each individual must then state their personal philosophy. 

Statement 1: All animal research should be allowed if a case can be made that the results might benefit humans.

Statement 2: No animal research should be permitted because animals cannot provide informed consent.

Statement 3: Not all human research should be allowed even if informed consent is given.
