If the string on the right was suddenly cut, the beam would accelerate due to gravity. What would be the tension in the string on the left at the instant when the right string is cut?

Solution Section:

The center of gravity is located at the balancing point where the sum of the moments is zero. One way to calculate this distance from the left end of the beam is to find the distance (X) where the entire mass (2 + 4 = 6 kg) could be located that would have the same moment as the two masses with their original locations. We then isolate X on one side by itself and solve. If the beam was supported by a knife edge placed 1.667 m from the left end of the beam, the moment would be zero and the beam would be perfectly balanced without the need of any other support.
