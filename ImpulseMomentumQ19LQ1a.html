Question 19

Forensic investigators often need to know the velocity of a bullet but it is difficult to measure. They use an experiment in which they fire a slug into a soft clay block that is suspended  and allowed to swing as a frictionless pendulum. Knowing the mass of the bullet, the mass of the block and the height that the block achieves after impact, they can determine the velocity of the bullet. How fast was the bullet going when it contacted the block if the mass of the bullet is 4.2 grams, the mass of the block is 1.928 kg and the height (h) is 0.215 m?

Solution Section:

Potential energy is equal to mass times gravity times height. In this case, the bullet increased the height of itself and the 1.928 kg block to a height of 0.215 m. The mass of the bullet is 0.0042 kg (4.2 grams).
