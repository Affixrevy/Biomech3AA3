Muscle Architecture

In the previous lecture we saw that muscle is a rather unique motor that develops force that is dependent on the activation as well as its length, velocity and history.  In this lecture, we look at how the muscles are arranged in the human musculo-skeletal system that affects the moments of force about joint centers and also how sarcomeres are arranged and interact with tendon properties to produce different movement outcomes.

Muscle Arrangement and Natural Pulleys:

It is common knowledge that the patella forms a natural pulley for the quadriceps muscle group that increases the moment of force about the human knee joint.  However,  most muscles in the human body do not exert force in a straight line between origin and insertion.  The path of most muscles travels over bony prominances, other muscles, or is re-directed by retinacula. The figure below (left) shows how the patella keeps the moment arm of the quadripceps relatively constant over a wide range of motion.  On the right, we see that the biceps moment arm decreases as the elbow extends and then becomes constant as the muscle bends over the bones, brachialis and other tissues beneath it.

Muscle moment arms throughout the joint range of motion are not easily measured.  Medical imaging (MRI, CT, etc.) has been used as well as models.
