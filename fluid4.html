Fluid Mechanics (continued)

Magnus Effect: The influence that rotation has on the path followed by a body travelling through a fluid.

The spin alters the velocity of the air flow on one side of the object relative to the other side. This results in a pressure difference in which there is a lower pressure on the side of higher velocity. The pressure difference causes a force in the direction of the low pressure. In the example above, the force causes the ball to follow a path that has a greater downward acceleration than with gravity alone and results in the ball not travelling as far before it contacts the ground. Under spin (or back spin) would have the opposite effect.
